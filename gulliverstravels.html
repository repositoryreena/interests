Gulliver's Travels

Author: Jonathan Swift
Published: 1726
Genre: Satire, Adventure
Synopsis:

*Gulliver's Travels* is a satirical novel by Jonathan Swift that follows the adventures of Lemuel Gulliver, an Englishman who embarks on several fantastical journeys to far-off lands. Each of these travels exposes the absurdities and flaws in human nature, government, and society. Through his encounters with miniature people, giant beings, and strange creatures, Gulliver's perspective on humanity becomes increasingly cynical.

The novel is a sharp critique of the politics, culture, and institutions of Swift's time, and it remains a classic work of English literature for its wit, imagination, and political commentary.
