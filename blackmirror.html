Black Mirror: Conformity and Society

Episodes Where Conformity is Questioned

Nosedive (Season 3, Episode 1)
Synopsis:

In a society where everyone is rated by others on a 5-star scale, Lacie, a woman obsessed with improving her social score, tries to climb the social ladder. As her rating plummets, she begins to unravel, revealing the dehumanizing effects of social conformity and the superficial pursuit of status.

Fifteen Million Merits (Season 1, Episode 2)
Synopsis:

In a future where people must earn "merits" to survive, the protagonist, Bing, fights against a system that demands constant consumption and conformity. The episode critiques consumerism, the exploitation of individuals, and the pressure to conform to the entertainment industry’s ideals.

White Bear (Season 2, Episode 2)
Synopsis:

Victoria wakes up with no memory of who she is, and soon finds herself being hunted by a mysterious mob. The episode critiques society’s obsession with punishment and spectacle, questioning the morality of societal systems built on conformity and voyeurism.

Hated in the Nation (Season 3, Episode 6)
Synopsis:

After a controversial figure is murdered, social media users are given the power to "vote" for individuals they want to see punished, leading to a breakdown in social justice. The episode critiques the dangers of mob mentality, online shaming, and the conformity of societal opinions based on viral trends.

Be Right Back (Season 2, Episode 1)
Synopsis:

After the death of her partner, Martha turns to a service that allows people to communicate with AI versions of their loved ones, who are constructed from their online footprints. The episode critiques how digital identities can replace authentic human connection and the pressures to conform to idealized versions of others.

The Entire History of You (Season 1, Episode 3)
Synopsis:

In a society where people can replay every moment of their lives through an implanted device, the protagonist becomes obsessed with his own memories and starts to unravel the consequences of living in a world where nothing is private, and personal boundaries are continuously violated.

Black Museum (Season 4, Episode 6)
Synopsis:

The episode explores the dark consequences of technological advances, particularly focusing on how people can be forced to conform to oppressive systems or manipulated for the sake of profit or entertainment. It critiques both the corporate control of personal lives and the loss of individuality in the face of exploitation.

Demon 79 (Season 6, Episode 5)
Synopsis:

In 1979, an Indian woman named Nida works in a department store and finds herself caught in a dilemma where she must decide whether to follow an otherworldly force’s demands or defy it. The episode explores how societal pressures—such as the expectations of a traditional culture, gender norms, and workplace conformity—can drive individuals to make extreme choices. It highlights the internal and external forces that shape one’s decisions in a world that often values obedience over individuality.
