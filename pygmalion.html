Pygmalion and Julie Andrews' Singing Legacy

Pygmalion, the play by George Bernard Shaw, was the inspiration for the beloved musical My Fair Lady. While Audrey Hepburn portrayed the lead role of Eliza Doolittle in the film adaptation, it was Julie Andrews who famously provided the singing voice for her. This behind-the-scenes connection mirrors the dynamic in Singin' in the Rain, where Kathy Selden (played by Debbie Reynolds) sings for the silent film star Lina Lamont (Jean Hagen). Just as Kathy’s voice becomes a stand-in for Lina’s onscreen persona, Julie Andrews’ voice became the unseen force behind Audrey Hepburn's portrayal of Eliza, transforming her performance into something more musical and iconic.
