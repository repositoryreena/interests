The Graduate

*The Graduate* (1967), directed by Mike Nichols, is a classic film about Benjamin Braddock, a recent college graduate who finds himself in an affair with the older Mrs. Robinson. The movie explores themes of confusion, rebellion, and the desire to avoid facing the responsibilities of adulthood.

What resonates with me from *The Graduate* is Benjamin's avoidance of the pressures around him. At one point, Mrs. Robinson famously asks him, "What are you doing here?" to which Benjamin responds, "I don't know." This uncertainty and lack of direction mirror how I sometimes feel about the expectations placed on me, particularly in terms of career and life choices.
