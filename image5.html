Little Miss Sunshine

"I wish I could just sleep until I was eighteen and skip all this crap-high school and everything-just skip it. Do you know who Marcel Proust is? He's the guy you teach. Yeah. French writer. Total loser. Never had a real job. Unrequited love affairs. Gay. Spent 20 years writing a book almost no one reads. But he's also probably the greatest writer since Shakespeare. Anyway, he uh... he gets down to the end of his life, and he looks back and decides that all those years he suffered, Those were the best years of his life, 'cause they made him who he was. All those years he was happy? You know, total waste. Didn't learn a thing. So, if you sleep until you're 18... Ah, think of the suffering you're gonna miss. I mean high school? High school-those are your prime suffering years. You don't get better suffering than that."

This quote reflects a feeling of frustration with life’s challenges, especially those of adolescence. Dwayne's contemplation on the value of suffering suggests that hardship, though painful, is a crucial part of growing and shaping one's identity. It highlights a sense of resignation but also an understanding that growth often comes from struggle.

Girl on the Bridge

"Learn to lose, or you'll take winning for granted."

This quote speaks to the inevitability of loss and failure. It suggests that experiencing defeat can help us appreciate success more deeply. The wisdom here is that without loss, we would not understand the true value of achievement, and the highs wouldn’t feel as meaningful.

The Virgin Suicides

"We felt the imprisonment of being a girl."

This quote from *The Virgin Suicides* captures the feeling of entrapment and repression that many young women experience. The sentiment speaks to the societal constraints placed on women, where their identity and choices are often restricted by external expectations.

Silver Linings Playbook

"Humanity is just nasty and there's no silver lining."

Tiffany’s quote reflects a disillusionment with the world, acknowledging the harshness of humanity. It highlights a period of emotional numbness or anger, where finding hope or positivity feels nearly impossible.

Dear Evan Hansen

"Even when the dark comes crashing through, and when you need a friend to carry you, when you're broken on the ground you will be found."

This quote embodies the theme of connection and support. It suggests that even in our lowest points, there is hope and a promise that others will help us find our way, reinforcing the importance of human connection and empathy.

Slumdog Millionaire

"It is written."

This quote speaks to fate and destiny, reflecting a sense of inevitability in life. It highlights the belief that certain events are preordained, and despite the trials faced, they lead us to where we are meant to be.

Suicide Squad

"Would you die for me? Yes. That's too easy. Would you live for me?"

This quote delves into the complexities of love and sacrifice. It challenges the idea of dramatic gestures and instead asks whether one can truly endure the hardship and commitment of living for someone, an exploration of loyalty and endurance.

Heathers

"What’s your damage, Heather?"
Teen angst meets homicidal fixation.

Sabrina

"A woman happily in love, she burns the soufflé. A woman unhappily in love, she forgets to turn on the oven."

This quote humorously reflects the emotional rollercoaster that love can bring. It contrasts the actions of a woman who is fulfilled in love versus one who is heartbroken, showing how deeply our emotions affect even the smallest aspects of life.

Secretary

"I want to be bored."

This quote speaks to the desire for peace and stability in a world full of emotional turmoil. It reflects a longing for simplicity and the relief that comes from escaping the intensity of one's emotions, choosing to embrace dullness over chaos.
