The Catcher in the Rye

Author: J.D. Salinger

Published: 1951

Genre: Realistic Fiction, Coming-of-Age

Synopsis:

*The Catcher in the Rye* follows the story of Holden Caulfield, a troubled teenager who has just been expelled from his prep school. Narrated by Holden himself, the novel takes the reader through his wandering days in New York City as he struggles with depression, alienation, and a deep sense of disillusionment with the adult world. His cynical view of life and his obsession with protecting the innocence of children are central themes in this landmark novel about adolescence and identity.
