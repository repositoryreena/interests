Sherlock: "Will Caring About Them Help Save Them?"

One of the most memorable moments in the BBC's *Sherlock* occurs when Dr. John Watson confronts Sherlock Holmes about his lack of empathy. In a tense scene, John says: "You don't care about people."

Sherlock responds, "Will caring about them help save them?" to which John replies, "No." This exchange perfectly captures the essence of Sherlock's character—his prioritization of logic and deduction over emotions.

Sherlock may seem cold and detached, but his ultimate goal is always to solve the case and prevent harm. His disdain for sentimentality is a defense mechanism, allowing him to focus on the facts without getting emotionally entangled. However, this does not mean he is immune to human connection—he just expresses it in his own unique way.
