Impressionable

Black Swan

"I was perfect."

The character of Nina in *Black Swan* pushes herself to the extreme in the pursuit of perfection. The quote reflects her internal struggle and the dangerous toll that the quest for flawlessness can have on her psyche.

The Little Mermaid

"Wish I could be part of that world."

Ariel's quote in *The Little Mermaid* reflects her longing to be part of the human world, not just because of a desire for freedom, but also because of her love for Prince Eric. Her wish to be "part of that world" expresses her deep emotional connection to him and the sacrifices she is willing to make to be with him.

Mean Girls

"It's not my fault you're, like, in love with me or something!"

In *Mean Girls*, Cady says the line "It's not my fault you're, like, in love with me or something!" to Janis, but this is a reflection of Cady's growing sense of superiority and her mistaken belief that everyone around her is infatuated with her. Cady says this to Janis without recognizing that Janis doesn't have romantic feelings for her at all. Instead, it’s more about Cady's increasing self-absorption and misunderstanding of the people around her as she becomes entangled in the social dynamics of high school.

Spirited Away

"I don’t want to be here. I want to go home."

This quote reflects Chihiro's spoiled and entitled attitude at the start of her journey. She is reluctant to accept the unfamiliar world she's been thrust into and is more focused on escaping to the comfort of her old life. Her character is impressionable, as she learns to mature and face challenges in the spirit world, leaving behind her initial selfishness.

The Devil Wears Prada

"Everybody wants to be us."

Miranda Priestly's line in *The Devil Wears Prada* reflects her power and influence in the fashion world, highlighting her belief in her own importance and superiority.

Coraline

"I wish I had a better life."

Coraline's desire for a better life mirrors her dissatisfaction with the ordinary and her yearning for something more magical or fulfilling, which is central to her journey in the story.

Vivarium

"Screaming"

This quote refers to the alien child in *Vivarium* who screams in a menacing and unsettling manner. The child is not just a victim of its environment but an evil force that manipulates and tortures the main characters, further highlighting the disturbing nature of the film. The child's screams are a constant reminder of its unnatural, malevolent existence.

Ella Enchanted

"I am not a doll to be dressed up and played with."

Ella's quote in *Ella Enchanted* challenges the expectations placed on her and her desire for autonomy, emphasizing the theme of self-determination and breaking free from societal expectations.

The Giver

"If everything’s the same, then there aren’t any choices!"

This line from *The Giver* reflects the novel's exploration of conformity, control, and the loss of individuality in a society that eliminates choice and freedom.

Memoirs of a Geisha

"My mother always said my sister, Satsu was like wood. As rooted to the earth as a sakura tree... But she told me I was like water... Water can carve its way through stone. And when trapped, water makes a new path."

This quote from *Memoirs of a Geisha* symbolizes the contrast between the two sisters and the strength of adaptability, with the narrator portraying water as an unstoppable force in the face of challenges.
