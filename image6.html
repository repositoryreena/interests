Candide by Voltaire

*Candide* is a satirical novella written by French philosopher Voltaire in 1759. The story follows the young and naïve Candide as he travels the world, experiencing a series of misadventures that expose the absurdities of human suffering, blind optimism, and the concept of "the best of all possible worlds." Through his encounters, Voltaire critiques optimism, religion, and society, particularly the idea that everything happens for the best in a world that often seems unjust.

As a determinist, I resonate with Voltaire's critique of blind optimism. In a world where events often seem to unfold according to forces beyond our control, *Candide* shows how even our best efforts to make things better can be thwarted by the unpredictable and uncontrollable nature of existence.

"All is for the best, in the best of all possible worlds." — Pangloss, the optimist philosopher in *Candide*.
