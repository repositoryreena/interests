The Scarlet Letter

Author: Nathaniel Hawthorne
Published: 1850
Genre: Historical Fiction, Romance
Synopsis:

*The Scarlet Letter* is a novel by Nathaniel Hawthorne set in 17th century Puritan Massachusetts. The story follows Hester Prynne, a woman who is publicly shamed and forced to wear a scarlet "A" for committing adultery. Despite her public disgrace, Hester remains strong and steadfast, refusing to reveal the identity of her child's father. As the story unfolds, themes of sin, guilt, and redemption emerge as Hester, her lover, and the Puritan community are all impacted by the consequences of their actions.

The novel explores the harsh moral codes of the time, examining how individuals navigate personal suffering and societal judgment. It is often regarded as one of the earliest and most influential works of American literature.
