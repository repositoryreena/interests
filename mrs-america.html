Mrs. America

Explore the themes of *Mrs. America* through other related films and series that address gender roles, feminism, and societal expectations.

In these films, I actually found myself appreciating the 1950s housewife characters. There's something so captivating about the way they dress—especially the dresses, aprons, and the overall prim-and-proper appearance. It's almost like a visual representation of their roles in society: neat, composed, and often forced into a mold. This is particularly evident in the contrasting scene from *Don't Worry Darling*, where a woman in scrubs stands in stark contrast to the pristine, well-groomed 1950s housewives around her. It's like a clash between the past and present, between tradition and the evolving roles of women. There's a certain beauty in how these housewives express themselves through their attire, even if their lives are often restricted by the very norms their clothes represent.

Overall, I love housewives as characters in media. Their stories often reflect resilience, whether they choose to challenge their situations or simply exist within them. It's a kind of strength that is often overlooked, but I think it's one of the most powerful forms of femininity portrayed in media.

By the way, if you haven't seen the second season of *Why Women Kill*, it's absolutely brilliant. It takes the premise of women making extreme choices to new heights, offering deeper insights into the complexity of female experience. I highly recommend it!

Also, I really enjoyed *Senior Year*—it's such a fun and entertaining movie! The premise is hilarious, and it's just the right mix of comedy and heart. If you haven't seen it yet, I definitely recommend giving it a watch.
