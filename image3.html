Siddhartha

Author: Hermann Hesse
Published: 1922
Genre: Fiction, Philosophical novel
Synopsis:

*Siddhartha* is a philosophical novel by Hermann Hesse, originally published in 1922. It tells the story of Siddhartha, a young man on a quest for enlightenment in ancient India. The novel explores themes of self-discovery, the nature of reality, and the journey toward inner peace.

The story follows Siddhartha’s journey as he seeks to understand the nature of existence. After leaving his wealthy family and a life of privilege, he explores different paths: asceticism, wealth, love, and finally, a peaceful acceptance of life’s impermanence. The novel is a profound exploration of spiritual growth, questioning societal norms, and ultimately finding peace within oneself.

*Siddhartha* is considered one of Hesse’s most important works, and it continues to resonate with readers who seek a deeper understanding of the human condition and the quest for meaning in life.
