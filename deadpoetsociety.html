Dead Poets Society - Carpe Diem

*Dead Poets Society* is a powerful film about the importance of individuality and the pressures of conformity. Set in a conservative prep school, the story follows a group of students who are inspired by their unorthodox English teacher, John Keating. Keating teaches them to seize the day, or as he famously puts it, "Carpe Diem," urging them to live life to the fullest and follow their own path, not the one laid out for them by society or their parents.

The message of *Dead Poets Society* resonates with the idea of "YOLO" (You Only Live Once). It's about embracing the moment, challenging societal norms, and living authentically, even if it means going against the grain. The students struggle with the weight of their parents' expectations and the societal standards placed on them, but Keating encourages them to choose their own destiny.

One of the most memorable quotes from the film is: "The powerful play goes on, and you may contribute a verse." This line serves as a reminder that each individual has the power to leave their own mark on the world, and the importance of making choices that reflect their true selves.
