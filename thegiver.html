The Giver

Author: Lois Lowry
Published: 1993
Genre: Dystopian, Science Fiction, Young Adult
Synopsis:

*The Giver* is a dystopian novel by Lois Lowry, set in a society that appears perfect on the surface but has eliminated all pain, suffering, and choice in order to maintain harmony and control. The story follows Jonas, a young boy who is selected to be the "Receiver of Memory," a prestigious and secretive position in his community.

As Jonas begins his training with the Giver, an elderly man who holds the memories of the past, he learns the dark truths about the society he lives in, including the cost of the tranquility and sameness it has achieved. The novel explores themes of freedom, memory, individuality, and the moral dilemmas surrounding a society that prioritizes safety over emotion and personal choice.

*The Giver* has been praised for its thought-provoking ideas and its exploration of the importance of memory and choice in human life. It is the first book in a loose quartet, followed by *Gathering Blue*, *Messenger*, and *Son*.
