Twelve Angry Men

Director: Sidney Lumet
Released: 1957
Genre: Drama, Courtroom Drama, Psychological Thriller
Synopsis:

*Twelve Angry Men* is a classic courtroom drama that revolves around a jury deliberating the guilt or innocence of a young man accused of murder. Initially, all but one juror are convinced of the defendant's guilt, but Juror #8 (played by Henry Fonda) insists that there is reasonable doubt and urges the group to reexamine the evidence.

The film is a tense, character-driven exploration of justice, prejudice, and the complexity of human nature. Each juror brings their personal biases, experiences, and perspectives to the deliberation, leading to a profound examination of how individual convictions and prejudices influence decision-making.

*Twelve Angry Men* is renowned for its exploration of group dynamics, the moral responsibility of those involved in the justice system, and the power of one individual's voice in effecting change. It remains a powerful commentary on human nature, ethics, and the pursuit of truth.
