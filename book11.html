Brave New World

Author: Aldous Huxley

Published: 1932

Genre: Dystopian Fiction, Science Fiction

Synopsis:

*Brave New World* is a chilling dystopian novel that imagines a future society where human beings are mass-produced, conditioned from birth, and divided into rigid social classes. In this world, individuality and emotions are suppressed in favor of conformity, and happiness is maintained through the use of a drug called "soma." The novel follows Bernard Marx, an outcast who begins to question the values of this controlled society, and his interactions with John, the "savage," who has been raised outside of this artificial world. Huxley critiques the dangers of totalitarianism, consumerism, and the loss of personal freedom.
