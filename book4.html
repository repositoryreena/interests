To Kill a Mockingbird

Author: Harper Lee

Published: 1960

Genre: Southern Gothic, Bildungsroman

Synopsis:

*To Kill a Mockingbird* is a powerful exploration of racial injustice and moral growth in the American South during the 1930s. The story is narrated by Scout Finch, a young girl whose father, Atticus Finch, is a lawyer defending a Black man, Tom Robinson, accused of raping a white woman. The novel addresses themes of prejudice, empathy, and the loss of innocence as Scout and her brother, Jem, witness their father's unwavering commitment to justice in the face of overwhelming societal bias.
